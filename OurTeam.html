Our Team

Tina Pokuaah

Tina has over 10 years experience in children's rights and child protection role's. She has utilized her skills to develop a career as a women’s rights adviser and young women's workshop facilitator, specializing in advocating against gender based violence. Her passion for human rights and female empowerment, was her motivation for founding 5Girls Project. Moreover, Tina has a MA in Children Youth and International Development, BSc Psychology degree, a Diploma in Children and Young People as well as a range of specialist qualifications on gender based violence. She is passionate about being part of the movement and dialogue to reshaping Africa’s poor development trajectory and believes if given their rights to reach their full potential girls and women have the power, to transform and elevate Africa.

Leanne Warinda

Leeanne our programme analyst/manager was born and raised in Zimbabwe. She has a strong interest and is passionate about female empowerment and African development. Leeanne has had significant experience of volunteering as well as working with charitable organisations particularly in the fields of fundraising ,research, project & database management. Academically, she holds two degrees in Sociology (BA hons)and an (MSc) in International Development (specialized in poverty,inequality &development). As a young African woman raised in a 'developing nation', she witnessed and developed a great understanding of the challenges many young girls face in securing their rights and pursing their dreams. It has always been her greatest desire to ensure that these girls have a chance to create better lives and are in a position to contribute significantly to the development of their communities and Africa as a whole.

Ida Agyekum

Ida is a trainee life coach with additional qualifications in Psychology and a National Diploma in Health and Social Care. Ida previously worked in the family court services dealing with child protection cases, she then followed on to provide practical support to young women in care, who were at risk or had fallen victim to CSE (Child sexual exploitation). She has utilized her experience to co-found 5Girls Project in which she has been passionate about providing girls strategies to develop their self-esteem and confidence, to be able to achieve their personal development goals. Moreover, Ida has also been dedicated in developing girl’s creative skills and educating them on how they can turn their passion into sustainable business.
